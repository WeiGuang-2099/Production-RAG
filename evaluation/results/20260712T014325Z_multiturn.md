# Multi-turn follow-ups: raw vs condensed vs oracle (base6)

**Question:** the UI is a persistent chat but the backend was stateless —
follow-ups with pronouns/ellipsis ("what about its training cost?")
retrieved against the raw text and missed. How much does a fast-model
condense-question step recover?

**Verdict:** condensation lifts follow-up recall@5 from 0.778 to 1.000 and
MRR from 0.702 to 1.000 — matching the hand-written oracle (recall@5 1.000,
MRR 0.972) — at 846 ms p50 of added latency per follow-up turn.

Setup: 18 hand-written follow-up items derived from the 48-question set
(deliberate pronouns/ellipsis; 3 per paper), retrieval pinned to the best
ablation stage (hybrid + rerank, graph off), retrieval-only metrics
(`evaluation/metrics.py`), base6 corpus. Conditions: `raw` = the follow-up
as typed; `condensed` = the fast-model rewrite that production now uses;
`oracle` = a hand-written standalone reference (upper bound on what any
rewriter could achieve).

## Results (48-question metric definitions, 18 items)

| condition | recall@5 | mrr | hit@5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| raw       | 0.778 | 0.702 | 0.778 |
| condensed | 1.000 | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| oracle    | 1.000 | 0.972 | 1.000 |

Condense step latency: p50 845.8 ms, p95 4239.0 ms (one gpt-4o-mini call
per follow-up turn; single-turn requests pay nothing).

## Qualitative rewrites

- Good: `In what three settings is it evaluated?` -> `In what three settings
  is in-context learning evaluated?` (mt009) — "it" resolved across a
  4-turn history where the referent was named two exchanges earlier.
- Worst: `What is that technique exactly?` -> `What is the technique of
  prompting with reasoning examples in relation to solving math problems?`
  (mt017) — the rewrite describes the referent instead of naming it
  (oracle: "What is chain-of-thought prompting?"); retrieval still hit
  because the description carries the paper's own vocabulary.

## Honest limits

- Condensed matched the oracle here (and edged its MRR: 1.000 vs 0.972, one
  oracle query ranked its paper second) — on this dataset the fast model's
  rewrite quality is not the bottleneck. A raw score of 0.778 also shows
  many follow-ups carry enough distinctive terms to survive uncondensed;
  the condense step buys the last ~0.22 of recall and a large MRR gain.
- Rewrite-type follow-ups ("explain that more simply") are out of contract:
  generation never sees history, so they condense poorly by design.
- 18 items, single run — treat small deltas as noise; the raw-vs-condensed
  gap is the signal, not third-decimal differences.
- Fabricated client history can steer the rewrite but never becomes a fact
  source (generation only sees retrieved context); history turns matching
  injection heuristics are dropped before the condense prompt.
